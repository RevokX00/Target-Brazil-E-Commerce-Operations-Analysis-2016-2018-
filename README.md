# Target Brazil — E-Commerce Operations Analysis (2016–2018)

**Real Dataset | 99,441 Orders | 8 Datasets | 27 States | 3-Year Review**

---

## Overview

A full three-year strategic diagnostic of Target's e-commerce operations in Brazil. This analysis covers 99,441 orders placed between September 2016 and October 2018 — examining order growth trends, customer geography, freight economics, delivery performance, payment behaviour, and customer satisfaction across all 27 Brazilian states.

Every finding is grounded in data. Every recommendation connects directly to a specific number.

---

## Key Findings

- **+137% revenue growth** comparing Jan–Aug 2017 vs Jan–Aug 2018 — growing at 11x the Brazilian market rate
- **São Paulo drives 40%+ of all orders** — significant concentration risk for a nationally positioned brand
- **Northern states face freight costs 3–4x higher** than the southeast — a structural conversion barrier suppressing order volume in RR, AP, and AM
- **Delivery consistently beats estimates** — multiple states receiving orders 10–20 days early, with zero marketing value being captured
- **Afternoon (13–18h) is the peak shopping window** — most campaigns are likely timed to the wrong part of the day
- **4.09/5.0 average review score** across 99,224 reviews — strong baseline with a fixable 1-star cluster driven by delivery delays in high-freight states
- **Credit card dominates at 70%+** with installment behaviour reflecting Brazilian purchasing power dynamics
